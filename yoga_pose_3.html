Downward Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)

Instructions

  1. Begin on your hands and knees, with wrists directly under shoulders and your knees directly under your hips.
  2. Spread your fingers wide and press your palms into the mat. Engage your core and tuck your toes under.
  3. On an exhale, lift your knees off the mat and straighten your legs, coming into an inverted V-shape.
  4. Your hands should be shoulder-width apart and your feet hip-width apart. Press your heels towards the ground, but don't worry if they don't touch.
  5. Lengthen your spine and draw your shoulder blades down and away from your ears. Your head should be relaxed and your gaze directed towards your feet.
  6. Hold the pose for 5-10 breaths, then release by dropping your knees back down to the mat.
